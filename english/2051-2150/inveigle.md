inveigle
### Meaning
+ _vt_: To win over by coaxing, flattery, or artful talk
+ _vt_: To obtain by cajolery
    + __eg__: inveigled a free pass to museum

### Origin

[Middle English envegle, alteration of Old French aveugler, to blind, from aveugle, blind, from Vulgar Latin *aboculus: Latin ab-, away from; see ab-1 + Latin oculus, eye (probably loan-translation of Gaulish exsops: exs-, from + ops, eye); See ok ∑- in Indo-European Roots.]

### Synonym

__eg__: he was attempting to inveigle them into doing his will
