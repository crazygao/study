inaugurate
### Meaning
+ _vt_: To induct into office by a formal ceremony
+ _vt_: To cause to begin, especially officially or formally
    + __eg__: inaugurate a new immigration policy
+ _vt_: To open or begin use of formally with a ceremony; dedicate
    + __eg__: inaugurate a community center

### Origin

[Latin inaugurāre, inaugurāt-, to consecrate by augury : in-, intensive pref.; see in-2 + augurāre, to augur (from augur, soothsayer. See aug- in Indo-European Roots).]

### Synonym

__eg__: he inaugurated a new policy of trade and exploration

+ INITIATE
+ opposite(s): END, WIND UP

__eg__: the new President will be inaugurated in January

+ ADMIT TO OFFICE

__eg__: the museum was inaugurated on September 12

+ OPEN
+ opposite(s): CLOSE
