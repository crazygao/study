plait
### Meaning
+ _noun_: A braid, especially of hair
+ _noun_: A pleat

+ _vt_: To braid
+ _vt_: To pleat
+ _vt_: To make by braiding

### Origin

Middle English pleit, fold, braid, possibly from pleiten, to fold, braid, alteration (influenced by Old French pleit, fold), of Old French plier, pleiir from Latin plicÄre, to fold. See plek- in Indo-European Roots
