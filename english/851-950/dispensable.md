dispensable
### Meaning
+ _adj_: Not essential; unimportant
	+ __eg__: dispensable items of personal property
+ _adj_: Capable of being dispensed, administered, or distributed
	+ __eg__: dispensable drugs
+ _adj_: Subject to dispensation, as a vow or church law

### Origin

[dispensable] early 16th cent. (in the sense ‘acceptable in special circumstances’): from medieval Latin dispensabilis, from Latin dispensare ‘continue to weigh out or disburse’, from the verb dispendere, based on pendere ‘weigh’

### Synonym

__eg__: he regards all of his lieutenants as highly dispensable

+ EXPENDABLE
+ opposite(s): INDISPENSABLE
