mollify
### Meaning
+ _vt_: To calm in temper or feeling; soothe
+ _vt_: To lessen in intensity; temper
+ _vt_: To reduce the rigidity of; soften

### Origin

Middle English mollifien, from Old French mollifier, from Late Latin mollificāre: Latin mollis, soft; See mel-1 in Indo-European Roots + -ficāre, -fy.

### Synonym

__eg__: nature reserves were set up to mollify local conservationists

+ APPEASE
+ opposite(s): ENRAGE

__eg__: the government's undertaking mollified the fears of the public

+ ALLAY
+ opposite(s): INFLAME, AGGRAVATE
