fiasco
### Meaning
+ _noun_: A complete failure

### Origin

[French, from Italian fare fiasco, to make a bottle, fail, from fiasco, bottle (perhaps translation of French bouteille, bottle, error, used by the French for linguistic errors committed by Italian actors on the 18th-century French stage), from Late Latin flasc≈ç. See flask.]

### Synonym

__eg__: the whole thing was a total fiasco

+ FAILURE
+ opposite(s): SUCCESS
