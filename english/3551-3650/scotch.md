scotch
### Meaning
+ _vt_: To put an abrupt end to
    + __eg__: The prime minister scotched the rumors of her illness with a public appearance
+ _vt_: To injure so as to render harmless
+ _vt_: To cut or score

+ _noun_: A surface cut or abrasion
+ _noun_: A line drawn on the ground, as one used in playing hopscotch

### Origin

Middle English scocchen, to cut, perhaps from Anglo-Norman escocher, to notch : es-, intensive pref. (from Latin ex-. See ex-) + Old French coche, notch (probably from Latin coccum, scarlet oak berry, from Greek kokkos).

### Synonym

__eg__: their plans were scotched by the Pentagon
